About PMEGP

The Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) is a credit-linked subsidy scheme aimed at generating employment opportunities by setting up micro-enterprises in rural and urban areas. It is implemented by the Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) at the national level.

Challenges of PMEGP

Due to structural issues and a high percentage of non-performing assets (NPAs), the scheme faces challenges. Between 2015–16 and 2019–20, Rs. 10,169 crores in aid was disbursed, of which Rs. 1,537 crores turned into NPAs. The primary reasons include lack of expertise, insufficient market research, limited demand, and intense competition.

Way Forward

The government must provide comprehensive training programs to help entrepreneurs focus on the right markets and products. Timely disbursement of funds is crucial for project completion and employment generation. Additionally, marketing support and better technologies for the micro-segment are essential.

AIM OF PMEGP

The primary objective of the PMEGP is to generate employment opportunities in rural and urban areas. It aims to provide sustainable employment to traditional artisans and youth, increase wage-earning capacity, and contribute to economic growth.
